History

Explore the rich history of our institution, from its humble beginnings to its growth into a beacon of academic excellence and community service.

Founding

St. Ferdinand College, a Catholic educational institution, was founded on September 28, 1950 by Msgr. Ricardo A. Jamias and Judge Manuel Arranz who were members of the Knight of Columbus IIagan Center, now Council 3705, Dr. Jose Valdez and Msgr. Alejandro Olalia. The Articles of incorporation was signed on December 21, 1950. During the mid-1990s, the school expanded its academic offerings and infrastructure. It launched BS Biology and BS Nutrition and Dietetics in 1996, and later BS Computer Science, Information Technology, and Information Management in SY 1999-2000.
The College of Education added new majors, while the College of Arts and Sciences introduced AB Political Science, Economics, and Philosophy.
The College of Commerce expanded with a BSC major in Business Administration, and the Graduate School began offering Master of Arts in English and Mathematics. Legal education was introduced with a Bachelor of Laws in SY 2000-2001.
Infrastructure improvements included a 4-story high school and elementary building, and renovations for Nursing and Midwifery programs.
Accreditation efforts began in 2000, achieving Level I and Level II statuses for various programs between 2001 and 2005, including Education, Liberal Arts, Commerce, Accountancy, Nursing, and Criminology.
