Patron Saint

Honoring the legacy of our patron saint, a symbol of faith, leadership, and dedication to education and service.

King Ferdinand the III

Saint Ferdinand III of Castile (c. 1199–1252) was the son of Alphonso IX of León and Berengaria of Castile. He became king of Castile in 1217 and León in 1230, uniting the two kingdoms. A devout and wise ruler, Ferdinand campaigned against the Moors for 27 years, reclaiming Andalusian territories like Córdoba (1236) and Seville (1249). Known for his military and administrative skills, he also pursued tolerance, encouraging conversions and founding the University of Salamanca in 1243. Ferdinand rebuilt Burgos Cathedral and converted Seville's mosque into a church. He married twice, with his daughter Eleanor becoming Queen of England. Buried in a Franciscan habit in Seville Cathedral, he was canonized in 1671. Depicted as a knight with a greyhound, Ferdinand is the patron saint of rulers, engineers, the Spanish army, the poor, and prisoners.
